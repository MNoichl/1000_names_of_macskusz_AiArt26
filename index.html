I — Origin

The First Macskusz

The original Macskusz is a nickname that Vero invented for Max in 2021. The name combines the Hungarian word macska (cat) with the Latin suffix -us.

The first illustration of Macskusz was created later that same year, around All Saints' Day, as a paper cutout. At the time, Vero had developed a strong interest in the Mexican craft tradition of papel picado.

The original Macskusz, 2021

II — The Book

A Diary of
Paper & Scissors

This first Macskusz was followed by a series of cutouts made from magazine scraps, which were pasted into a specially made book that functioned much like a diary.

III — The Names

Avatars &
Manifestations

The individual Macskusz avatars, or "names," reflect different aspects of Max and Vero's relationship: shared experiences, arguments, and images drawn from the media they consumed together.

Over time, the avatars quickly drifted away from the original feline form. In some cases they are no longer creatures at all, but resemble natural forces or landscapes.

Selected paper-cut avatars from the book series, 2021

IV — Digital Revival

Macskuszisation
of the World

The paper-cut series was still limited to the pages of the book. Although the project concluded with image no. 76 in the same year, it was revived in 2025 when Max proposed recreating the process of the "Macskuszisation" of the world using digital methods.

V — Generated Avatars

A New Name
Every Day

The work consists of a series of images automatically generated each day. With every update, the images present the current daily version of an avatar of Macskusz — a mythical being that emerged from the love language between Max and Vero and gradually took on a life of its own.

It functions both as a mascot and as a form of documentation of their relationship, attempting to capture the experiences and moods of each day by allowing them to manifest, day after day, in a new entity.

VI — The Exhibition

Exhibition
Proposal

For the exhibition, we propose presenting a mini-series of three Macskusz avatars. These avatars are newly generated each day and appear at noon (12:00 PM in Denver).

For the presentation, we require a screen displaying our website, where the current image will be shown.
